# How to Use This Site

## Quick Start Guide

This annotated edition of the EU AI Act is designed for easy navigation and comprehensive understanding. Here's how to get the most out of it.

## 🔍 Finding Information

### Using Search

The search bar at the top of every page is your fastest way to find information:

1. **Click the search icon** or press `/` on your keyboard
2. **Type your query** - Examples:
   - "high-risk" → Find all references to high-risk AI
   - "Article 6" → Jump directly to Article 6
   - "prohibited" → See prohibited practices
   - "GDPR" → Find GDPR cross-references
3. **Select from results** - Click any result to jump there immediately

!!! tip "Search Tips"
    - Use specific terms for better results
    - Search works across articles, recitals, and commentary
    - Results show context snippets
    - Search suggestions appear as you type

### Navigation Structure

The site is organized hierarchically:

```
Home
├── Recitals (138 recitals)
├── Articles
│   ├── Chapter I (General Provisions)
│   ├── Chapter II (Prohibited Practices)
│   ├── Chapter III (High-Risk Systems)
│   └── ... (Chapters IV-XIII)
├── Annexes (I-XIII)
├── Cross-References
│   ├── GDPR Mapping
│   ├── DSA Mapping
│   └── Other regulations
├── Guidance
│   ├── Compliance Checklist
│   ├── Timeline
│   └── Industry Guides
└── Resources
    ├── Glossary
    ├── FAQ
    └── External Links
```

### Breadcrumbs

At the top of each page, breadcrumbs show your location:

`Home > Articles > Chapter III > Article 6`

Click any breadcrumb to navigate back up the hierarchy.

## 📄 Understanding Page Layout

### Reading Strategies

#### 1. **Top-Down Approach**
Start with Chapter I, read sequentially through all articles.

**Best for:** First-time readers, comprehensive understanding

#### 2. **Issue-Specific Approach**
Use search to find relevant provisions for your specific concern.

**Best for:** Practitioners addressing specific questions

#### 3. **Chapter-by-Chapter**
Focus on one chapter at a time, reading all articles and recitals.

**Best for:** Studying specific topics (e.g., high-risk requirements)

#### 4. **Cross-Reference Following**
Start with one article, follow all its cross-references.

**Best for:** Understanding interconnections and impact
